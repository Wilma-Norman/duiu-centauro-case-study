The Story

What happened during the Drop

21 days. 11,400 creators. One Gold tier unlocked.

The Brief

Centauro came to DUIU looking for something traditional media could not give them: proof that real people loved their gear. The brief was simple. Open a Drop called "Seu Melhor Jogo," set a 15,000 BRL Gold prize pool, and invite Brazil to show their best sports moment while wearing Centauro. No script. No casting. Just the community.

What Happened

The Drop opened on a Tuesday morning and hit 1,000 Replies by Friday. By day eight, a clip from a 17-year-old futsal player in Recife had collected over 28,000 votes and was being shared across group chats without any paid distribution behind it. The invite multiplier climbed to 1.6x, meaning more than half the new participants arrived through a direct share from someone already inside the campaign. Centauro's team watched in real time as the Thread filled with content they could never have briefed for or bought.

The Outcome

The Gold prize tier unlocked on day fourteen, three days ahead of the campaign's expected pace. By the close, 2.5 million unique accounts had seen at least one Reply, with 41 percent of participants returning to vote or engage on more than one session. Centauro left with a library of authentic user-generated content, a measurable community they did not have before the Drop, and a media cost per engaged viewer that their team described as unlike anything in their current mix.

"We briefed for a campaign. What we got was a community. The content people made for us was better than anything we could have produced ourselves."

Head of Brand, Centauro — Post-campaign debrief
